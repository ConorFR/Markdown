Soil chemistry characteristics were compared across treatments and sites (Table 1.1). Soil inorganic C was only found to be above the minimum threshold of 0.1 for 6 samples and was ignored. Soil textural classifications for individual samples can be found in Supplementary Table 4.1. Fitting the data to two-way ANOVA models of site and treatment variables found the data to be non-normally distributed and inequal in variances across both variable when investigating each chemistry characteristic individually. Therefore, kruskall-wallis tests were used to compare differences in the chemistry characteristics across sites, and across treatments. After adjusting for multiple testing using the Benjamini-Hochberg method, all chemical characteristics were found to differ significantly by site, and all characteristics except for K mg/mL were found to vary significantly across treatment conditions. The results of normality, homoscedasticity, and kruskall-wallis tests of both site and treatment variables can be found in Supplementary table 4.2. Inspecting the chemistry across sites and treatments reveals that mature treatment plots appeared consistently different from the other treatments (Supplementary figure 4.1).
